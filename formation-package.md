## **Section 3: Federal Tax-Exempt Status**

### **Form 1023 vs 1023-EZ**

If you meet ALL of the following, you can use the simpler Form 1023-EZ:

* Projected annual gross receipts ≤ $50,000 for each of first 3 years

* Total assets ≤ $250,000

* Not a school, hospital, or supporting organization

* Not a successor to a for-profit entity

| Form 1023-EZ | Form 1023 (Full) |
| ----- | ----- |
| • Filing fee: $275 • 3-page form • Processing: 2-4 weeks • Must file online at pay.gov | • Filing fee: $600 • 28+ pages plus attachments • Processing: 3-6 months • More detailed scrutiny |

* ☐  Apply for EIN (Employer Identification Number) — irs.gov, free, immediate

* ☐  Complete Form 1023 or 1023-EZ eligibility worksheet

* ☐  Gather required attachments: Articles, Bylaws, conflict of interest policy

* ☐  File Form 1023 or 1023-EZ at pay.gov

* ☐  Receive IRS Determination Letter (keep forever)

**BYLAWS**

OF

**THE CREATIVE ADVANCE INSTITUTE**

## **ARTICLE I: NAME AND PURPOSE**

**Section 1.1 Name**

The name of the organization is The Creative Advance Institute (the "Corporation").

**Section 1.2 Purpose**

The Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes as described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. The mission of the Corporation is to steward and scale The Threads of Becoming, an open-source framework for conflict transformation, by providing education, training, and community for navigating creative tension as a source of innovation and growth.

## **ARTICLE II: OFFICES**

**Section 2.1 Principal Office**

The principal office of the Corporation shall be located at the address designated by the Board of Directors.

**Section 2.2 Other Offices**

The Corporation may have offices at such other places as the Board of Directors may from time to time determine.

## **ARTICLE III: BOARD OF DIRECTORS**

**Section 3.1 Powers**

The Board of Directors shall have all powers necessary or appropriate to manage the Corporation and carry out its purposes.

**Section 3.2 Number and Composition**

The Board shall consist of not less than three (3) nor more than nine (9) directors. The Board shall reflect diversity of background, expertise, and perspective, including representation from fields relevant to the Corporation's mission.

**Section 3.3 Terms**

Directors shall serve staggered three-year terms, with approximately one-third of the Board elected each year. Directors may serve up to two consecutive terms.

**Section 3.4 Election**

Directors shall be elected by the Board at the annual meeting.

**Section 3.5 Meetings**

The Board shall meet at least quarterly. Special meetings may be called by the Chair or by one-third of the directors. Directors may participate by telephone or video conference.

**Section 3.6 Quorum**

A majority of the Board shall constitute a quorum.

**Section 3.7 Voting**

Each director shall have one vote. Decisions shall be made by majority vote of those present at a meeting at which a quorum is present.

**Section 3.8 Removal**

A director may be removed with or without cause by a two-thirds vote of the remaining directors.

**Section 3.9 Vacancies**

Vacancies shall be filled by a majority vote of the remaining directors.

**Section 3.10 Committees**

The Board may establish committees as needed, including but not limited to:

* Executive Committee

* Finance Committee

* Program Committee

* Governance Committee

## **ARTICLE IV: OFFICERS**

**Section 4.1 Officers**

The officers of the Corporation shall be a Chair, a Vice Chair, a Secretary, and a Treasurer. The Board may appoint additional officers as needed.

**Section 4.2 Election and Term**

Officers shall be elected by the Board at the annual meeting and shall serve one-year terms. Officers may be re-elected.

**Section 4.3 Duties**

* Chair: Preside at meetings, execute contracts, oversee operations, serve as primary spokesperson.

* Vice Chair: Assume duties of Chair in their absence, assist Chair as needed.

* Secretary: Maintain records, minutes, and official documents; ensure proper notice of meetings.

* Treasurer: Oversee finances, budgets, and financial reporting; ensure proper financial controls.

## **ARTICLE V: EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR**

**Section 5.1 Appointment**

The Board may appoint an Executive Director to serve as the chief executive officer of the Corporation. The Executive Director shall serve at the pleasure of the Board.

**Section 5.2 Duties**

The Executive Director shall:

* Implement the policies and programs established by the Board;

* Manage the day-to-day operations of the Corporation;

* Hire, supervise, and discharge staff as authorized by the Board;

* Prepare budgets and financial reports for Board review;

* Serve as the primary liaison between the Board and staff;

* Represent the Corporation to the public and stakeholders;

* Perform such other duties as assigned by the Board.

**Section 5.3 Compensation**

The compensation of the Executive Director shall be determined by the Board. Any compensation arrangement shall be reviewed and approved by the Board in accordance with the Conflict of Interest Policy to ensure reasonableness.

**Section 5.4 Board Membership**

The Executive Director may attend Board meetings but shall not be a voting member of the Board unless separately elected as a director. If the Executive Director serves as a director, they shall recuse themselves from any vote regarding their own compensation or performance evaluation.

## **ARTICLE VI: FISCAL MANAGEMENT**

**Section 6.1 Fiscal Year**

The fiscal year shall be January 1 through December 31\.

**Section 6.2 Budget**

The Board shall approve an annual budget before the beginning of each fiscal year.

**Section 6.3 Financial Review**

The Corporation shall undergo an annual financial review by an independent accountant when annual revenues exceed $100,000, or as otherwise required by law or determined by the Board.

**Section 6.4 Contracts and Checks**

The Board shall designate which officers or employees may execute contracts and sign checks on behalf of the Corporation.

## **ARTICLE VII: INDEMNIFICATION**

The Corporation shall indemnify directors, officers, and employees to the fullest extent permitted by Florida law.

## **ARTICLE VIII: AMENDMENTS**

These Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds vote of the Board at any meeting, provided that notice of the proposed amendment was given with the meeting notice at least ten (10) days in advance.

# **CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY**

## **1\. Purpose**

To ensure that decisions made by the Board, officers, and key employees are in the best interest of the Corporation and comply with legal and ethical standards.

## **2\. Definitions**

A conflict of interest exists when a person's personal, financial, or professional interests could improperly influence their judgment in fulfilling their duties to the Corporation.

An "interested person" is any director, officer, or key employee who has a direct or indirect financial interest, as defined below.

A person has a "financial interest" if they have, directly or indirectly:

1) An ownership or investment interest in any entity with which the Corporation has a transaction or arrangement;

2) A compensation arrangement with the Corporation or with any entity with which the Corporation has a transaction or arrangement; or

3) A potential ownership or investment interest in, or compensation arrangement with, any entity with which the Corporation is negotiating a transaction or arrangement.

## **3\. Disclosure Requirements**

All directors, officers, and key employees shall annually complete a written disclosure statement identifying any relationships, positions, or circumstances in which they are involved that could contribute to a conflict of interest.

## **4\. Procedures**

When a conflict of interest is identified:

4) The interested person shall disclose all material facts related to the conflict;

5) The interested person shall leave the meeting during the discussion and vote on the matter;

6) The remaining disinterested members shall determine whether a conflict exists and whether the proposed transaction is fair and reasonable to the Corporation;

7) The disinterested members may approve the transaction only by a majority vote.

## **5\. Violations**

If the Board has reasonable cause to believe that an interested person has failed to disclose a conflict, it shall inform the person of the basis for such belief and afford them an opportunity to explain. If the Board determines that a violation has occurred, it shall take appropriate corrective action.

## **6\. Records**

The minutes of the Board shall contain:

8) The names of persons who disclosed a conflict or were found to have a conflict;

9) The nature of the conflict;

10) Any action taken to determine whether a conflict existed;

11) The Board's decision as to whether a conflict existed and whether the transaction was fair;

12) The names of persons present for discussions and votes.

## **7\. Annual Review**

This policy shall be reviewed annually by the Board.

Each director, officer, and key employee shall sign a statement affirming that they have received, read, and understand this policy and agree to comply with it.

# **WHISTLEBLOWER POLICY**

## **1\. Purpose**

To encourage reporting of suspected improper conduct without fear of retaliation, and to provide a process for addressing such concerns.

## **2\. Scope**

This policy applies to all directors, officers, employees, volunteers, and contractors of the Corporation.

## **3\. Reporting Procedure**

Anyone who reasonably believes that a violation of law, regulation, or Corporation policy has occurred or is about to occur should report the concern. Concerns may be reported to:

13) The Chair of the Board;

14) The Treasurer (for financial concerns);

15) Any member of the Board;

16) An anonymous third-party reporting service, if one is established by the Board.

If the concern involves the Chair, the report should be made to the Vice Chair or another Board member.

## **4\. Investigation**

All reports will be promptly and thoroughly investigated by an appropriate person or committee designated by the Board. The investigation will be conducted with appropriate confidentiality.

## **5\. No Retaliation**

No individual who in good faith reports a concern shall suffer harassment, retaliation, or adverse employment consequence. Anyone who retaliates against a reporter is subject to discipline, up to and including termination.

## **6\. Confidentiality**

Reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible consistent with the need to conduct a thorough investigation and comply with applicable law.

## **7\. False Reports**

Making a report known to be false or made with reckless disregard for the truth is prohibited and may result in disciplinary action.

## **8\. Documentation**

Documentation of all complaints and investigations shall be maintained in accordance with the Document Retention Policy.

# **DOCUMENT RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION POLICY**

## **1\. Purpose**

To ensure proper retention and destruction of documents in compliance with legal requirements and operational needs, and to provide guidance on what documents should be retained and for how long.

## **2\. Retention Schedule**

Documents shall be retained for the periods set forth below:

| Document Type | Retention Period |
| :---- | :---- |
| Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, amendments | Permanent |
| IRS Determination Letter | Permanent |
| Board meeting minutes | Permanent |
| Tax returns (Form 990\) | Permanent |
| Financial records, ledgers, audit reports | 7 years |
| Bank statements, canceled checks | 7 years |
| Contracts and agreements | 7 years after expiration |
| Employment records | 7 years after termination |
| Grant records and reports | 7 years after grant closure |
| Insurance policies | Permanent |
| Program materials and publications | As needed for ongoing use |
| General correspondence | 3 years |

## **3\. Destruction Method**

Confidential documents shall be destroyed by shredding or secure electronic deletion. Non-confidential documents may be recycled.

## **4\. Suspension of Destruction**

Document destruction shall be suspended immediately if the Corporation becomes aware of any litigation, government investigation, or audit. The Board Chair and legal counsel shall be notified immediately of any such matter.

## **5\. Electronic Records**

Electronic records are subject to the same retention requirements as paper records. Regular backups shall be maintained and tested.

## **6\. Implementation**

The Executive Director (or Chair, if no ED) shall be responsible for implementing this policy and ensuring compliance.

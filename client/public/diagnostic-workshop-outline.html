Organizational Thread Diagnostic Workshop

3-Hour Leadership Team Session

Workshop Overview

Purpose: Identify which threads are driving organizational tension and develop initial navigation strategies

Audience

Leadership teams (5-15 participants): C-suite executives, department heads, team leads

Format

Facilitated in-person or virtual session

Duration

3 hours (half-day)

Deliverables:
  • Thread map identifying primary and secondary tensions
  • Collapse pattern analysis
  • Initial navigation strategies (actionable first steps)
  • Custom diagnostic report (delivered within 5 business days)

Pre-Workshop Preparation

2 Weeks Before:

  • Leadership team completes Organizational Thread Assessment individually
  • Facilitator reviews results to identify likely primary threads
  • Participants submit 1-2 current tensions they want to address

1 Week Before:

  • Facilitator shares agenda and pre-reading:
    • Introduction to Seven Threads (10-minute read)
    • Assessment results summary (anonymized)
  • Optional: 15-minute 1:1 calls with key stakeholders

Workshop Agenda

Part 1: Foundations (45 minutes)

0:00-0:10 | Welcome & Framing

  • Introductions (if needed)
  • Workshop goals and structure
  • Ground rules: Curiosity over judgment, name patterns not blame, tensions are data not problems

0:10-0:30 | Seven Threads Framework

Interactive teaching segment covering:

  • Brief overview of all seven threads
  • Key concept: Tensions ≠ Problems (Problems have solutions; Tensions require navigation)
  • Collapse patterns: Oscillating, Stuck, Fragmented

0:30-0:45 | Assessment Results Review

  • Share aggregated assessment results
  • Identify threads with highest scores
  • Participants share: "Which thread description resonated most?"

Output: Prioritized list of 2-3 threads to explore

Part 2: Thread Mapping (90 minutes)

0:45-1:15 | Primary Thread Deep Dive

Facilitated mapping exercise in three steps:

Step 1: Name the Tension (10 min)

  • What is the specific tension your organization is navigating?
  • Frame as "X ↔ Y" (both/and, not either/or)
  • Example: "Predictability ↔ Adaptability" (likely PAUSE thread)

Step 2: Map the Poles (10 min)

  • What does full collapse into Pole A look like?
  • What does full collapse into Pole B look like?
  • What are the gifts of each pole? (Reframe from "good vs. bad")

Step 3: Identify Current Collapse Pattern (10 min)

  • Are we oscillating? (Swinging back and forth)
  • Are we stuck? (Collapsed into one pole)
  • Are we fragmented? (Different parts at different poles)

Output: Visual thread map on whiteboard/Miro

1:15-1:30 | Break

1:30-2:15 | Pattern Analysis

Explore the system beneath the symptoms:

  • When did this tension first emerge? What organizational change triggered it?
  • Who is advocating for each pole? Which teams/roles pull toward each side?
  • What reinforces the collapse? Structures, cultural norms, leadership behaviors
  • What would "holding both poles" look like? Can we imagine honoring both?

Output: Root cause analysis & systemic reinforcers identified

Part 3: Navigation Strategies (45 minutes)

2:15-2:45 | Design Initial Interventions

Framework: 3 Levels of Intervention

  1. Language: How do we name this tension going forward? Create thread shorthand for teams
  2. Structure: What organizational changes support holding both poles? (Decision rights, meeting rhythms, metrics, resource allocation)
  3. Practice: What daily/weekly practices reinforce navigation? (Check-ins, "both/and" prompts, retrospectives)

2:45-3:00 | Commit to Next Steps

Define immediate actions:

  • 30-day experiment: What will we try for the next month?
  • Who owns it: Assign clear accountability
  • How we'll know: Define success criteria
  • When we'll review: Schedule follow-up check-in

Output: Action plan with owners and timelines

Post-Workshop Deliverables

Within 5 Business Days:

Custom Diagnostic Report including:

  • Thread map (visual diagram)
  • Collapse pattern analysis
  • Recommended navigation strategies
  • 30/60/90-day roadmap
  • Resources for leadership team

Within 2 Weeks:

Follow-up call with leadership sponsor (30 min)

  • Review report
  • Clarify questions
  • Discuss integration options (if desired)

Facilitation Approach

Facilitator Role:
  • Pattern recognition: Help teams see what they're too close to see
  • Non-directive: Guide discovery, don't prescribe solutions
  • Thread fluency: Translate organizational dynamics into thread language
  • Conflict-comfortable: Hold space for disagreement without rushing to resolution

Key Facilitation Moves:

  1. Name the collapse in real-time – "Notice we just oscillated between planning and executing—that's the PAUSE thread showing up right now"
  2. Reframe from blame to pattern – Not "Engineering is always blocking us" but "Engineering is holding the 'Not Yet' pole; Product is holding the 'Now' pole—how do we honor both?"
  3. Invite embodied noticing – "Where do you feel this tension in your body when it shows up?"
  4. Hold paradox – Resist the urge to resolve too quickly; let the team sit in "both are true"

Customization Options

By Organization Size:

  • 5-10 people: Single-day intensive (6 hours) to go deeper
  • 15+ people: Multiple sessions with subset groups, then synthesis
  • Multi-site: Virtual workshop with breakout rooms by location

By Thread Focus:

  • Single-thread deep dive: If assessment clearly shows one primary thread
  • Multi-thread mapping: If organization has multiple threads in collapse

Add-ons:

  • +90 min: Include team-level thread mapping (beyond leadership)
  • +2 hours: Design facilitation toolkit for leaders to use with their teams
  • +Follow-up: Monthly thread navigation check-ins (ongoing)

Success Indicators

You'll know the workshop was effective if:

Immediate (end of session):
  • Team has shared language for naming the tension
  • Participants can identify collapse patterns in real-time
  • Leadership is aligned on primary thread(s)
  • Concrete 30-day experiment is defined
30 Days Post:
  • Teams are using thread language in meetings
  • Decisions reference "holding both poles"
  • Collapse patterns are named when they occur (not ignored)
90 Days Post:
  • Measurable shift in decision-making velocity or conflict duration
  • Leadership reports increased clarity on navigation
  • Organization is ready to integrate threads at scale (or knows it's not a fit)

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can we do this remotely?
Yes. Virtual workshops use Miro or Zoom whiteboards for collaborative mapping. Effectiveness is comparable to in-person when facilitated well.

Q: What if we're not sure which thread is primary?
That's what the workshop is designed to discover. Pre-assessment results guide us, but the live mapping often reveals unexpected patterns.

Q: Do we need the whole leadership team?
Ideally yes—decision-makers who can commit to navigation strategies. Minimum 5 people; maximum 15 for effective facilitation.

Q: What happens after the workshop?
You have three options:

  1. Take the insights and run with them (self-guided)
  2. Schedule follow-up sessions for ongoing navigation support
  3. Engage in full integration program (6-month partnership)

Q: How do we measure ROI?
Most organizations track:

  • Decision-making velocity (time from issue to resolution)
  • Meeting efficiency (time spent on recurring conflicts)
  • Employee sentiment (engagement scores related to clarity/alignment)
  • Leadership alignment (reduced escalations, faster strategic agreement)

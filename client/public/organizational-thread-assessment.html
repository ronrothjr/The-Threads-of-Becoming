Organizational Thread Assessment Tool

A diagnostic guide to identify which threads are driving tension in your organization

How to Use This Assessment

This tool helps leadership teams quickly identify which of the seven threads are currently in collapse or creating tension in your organization.

  1. Answer each question honestly based on current organizational dynamics
  2. Score each answer: Never (0) | Rarely (1) | Sometimes (2) | Often (3) | Constantly (4)
  3. Tally your scores for each thread (4 questions × 4 points = 16 points possible per thread)
  4. Focus on threads scoring 11 or higher — these are likely in collapse

Interpreting Your Results

High Scores (11-16): Thread in Collapse

This thread is currently driving significant organizational tension. Prioritize navigating this thread.

Medium Scores (6-10): Thread Under Tension

Watch this thread — it's not in full collapse but showing signs of strain.

Low Scores (0-5): Thread in Balance

This thread is currently well-navigated (or not yet activated as a tension point).

Next Steps

If you scored 11+ on ANY thread:

  1. Name it: Share this assessment with your leadership team. Use thread language to describe what's happening.
  2. Map the collapse pattern: Are you oscillating between extremes? Stuck at one pole? Use the diagnostic workshop to map the specific pattern.
  3. Design navigation: Don't try to "solve" the tension. Design practices that hold both poles simultaneously.

If you scored 11+ on MULTIPLE threads:

You likely have a systemic collapse pattern. One thread may be primary, with others as secondary effects.

Recommended: Schedule a diagnostic workshop to identify the core thread driving the system.

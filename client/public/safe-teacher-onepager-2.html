Teacher One-Pager #2

Using S.A.F.E. in the Moment • Purpose: Normalize partial use and speed
30-Second Framing (Read to Students)

"You don't stop class for S.A.F.E. You use it inside what's already happening."

2-Minute Explanation

"Most of the time, S.A.F.E. sounds like one sentence."

Examples teachers can say:

  • "I SEE something happening here."
  • "Can you ASK what they need?"
  • "Can you FEEL what's going on in your body?"
  • "Pause — who do you want to be right now?"
"That's it. You're not fixing the conflict. You're creating space."
2-Minute Practice

Teacher reads 3 quick scenarios, students answer with one S.A.F.E. word they'd use first.

Example:

  • "Two students arguing loudly" → SEE or PAUSE
  • "Someone looks upset but silent" → ASK
  • "You feel like yelling" → FEEL
30-Second Close

"If you only remember one word, that's enough."

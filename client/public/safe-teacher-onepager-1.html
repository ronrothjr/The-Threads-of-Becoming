Teacher One-Pager #1

What S.A.F.E. Is (and Isn't) • Purpose: Prevent misuse, fear, and overreach
30-Second Framing (Read to Students)

"We're going to learn a simple way to handle everyday conflicts — not emergencies. This is not about getting in trouble or fixing anyone. It's about learning how to pause and choose who you want to be when things get hard."

2-Minute Explanation

"S.A.F.E. has four steps. You don't have to use all of them every time. Sometimes you'll only use one."

  • SEE – Notice what's happening (outside or in your body)
  • ASK – Ask what someone is feeling or needing
  • FEEL – Name your own feeling or need
  • EMERGE – Choose how you want to act
"Important: If someone is in danger, an adult steps in immediately. S.A.F.E. is for everyday conflicts — not emergencies."
(This protects SEE & ACT without naming it yet.)
2-Minute Practice (Whole Class)

Teacher gives a neutral scenario: "Someone cuts in line."

Ask:

  • "What could you SEE?"
  • "What could you ASK?"
  • "What might you FEEL?"
  • "How could you EMERGE?"

No acting. Just answers.

30-Second Close

"We're not trying to be perfect. We're practicing noticing and choosing."
